# 📈 QuantX

> A quantitative trading strategy backtesting platform built with Python and Streamlit.

QuantX allows users to backtest multiple trading strategies, compare their performance against a benchmark, and analyze key financial metrics through an interactive dashboard.

---

## ✨ Features

### 📊 Single Strategy Dashboard
- Choose from multiple trading strategies
- Enter any stock ticker
- Configure initial investment capital
- Interactive equity curve visualization
- Benchmark comparison (Buy & Hold)

### 📈 Performance Metrics
- Total Return
- Sharpe Ratio
- Maximum Drawdown
- CAGR
- Volatility
- Win Rate

### 📋 Strategy Comparison
Compare multiple strategies side-by-side:

- Buy & Hold
- Moving Average
- Mean Reversion

Includes:
- Performance comparison table
- Winner detection
- Return comparison chart
- Sharpe Ratio comparison
- Drawdown comparison

### 📁 Export
Download strategy results as CSV for further analysis.

# 📊 Performance Metrics Explained

### Return
Overall percentage gain or loss generated by the strategy.

### Sharpe Ratio
Measures risk-adjusted returns. A higher Sharpe Ratio indicates better performance relative to risk.

### Maximum Drawdown
The largest decline from a portfolio's peak value.

### CAGR
Compound Annual Growth Rate representing annualized returns.

### Volatility
Annualized standard deviation of strategy returns.

### Win Rate
Percentage of profitable trading periods.

# 📈 Strategies Included

## Buy & Hold

Purchases the asset once and holds it throughout the investment period.

---

## Moving Average

Generates buy signals when price crosses above its moving average.

---

## Mean Reversion

Assumes prices tend to revert toward their historical average after significant deviations.
